# CANON — Product Memo v0.1

## One-line
CANON is a subscription product for legible human judgment: a living ledger of what one editor read, ignored, rejected, and changed their mind about.

## What it is
CANON is built on the idea that in an infinite content environment, the scarce resource is not information but competent refusal.

It treats editorial selection not as a hidden process and not as a platform black box, but as the product itself.

A subscriber does not primarily pay for essays, content volume, or hot takes.
They pay for visible discernment.

The core object is the judgment ledger.

## The problem
The systems that once performed large-scale filtering and selection have degraded:
- legacy editorial institutions are weaker, thinner, or economically misaligned
- algorithmic feeds optimize for engagement rather than judgment
- creators can monetize attention more easily than refusal
- readers are expected to perform their own triage in an environment of overwhelming abundance

This produces a new scarcity:
- believable selection
- trusted exclusion
- visible standards
- accountable judgment

People do not only need someone to tell them what matters.
They need someone to tell them what is not worth their finite life.

## Product thesis
There is a real market for legible, accountable judgment.

A useful editorial product in this environment should make visible:
- what an editor chose to engage
- what they refused
- why they ruled as they did
- where they changed their mind
- where their standards held or failed

The hidden labor of discernment should become inspectable and subscribable.

## Core product
Each CANON editor maintains a living ledger of judgment acts.

A judgment act includes:
- item
- action
- ruling
- confidence
- optional short Case
- timestamp

Possible actions:
- Read
- Pass
- Revisit

Possible rulings:
- Strengthens
- Complicates
- Breaks
- Unworthy
- Contempt (use sparingly; for bad-faith or deeply misleading material)

Example surface:
- Atlantic article on semiconductors — Read — Strengthens — High
- Viral nutrition thread — Pass — Breaks (methodology) — Absolute
- Obscure PDF on monetary policy — Read — Complicates — Medium
- Trending documentary — Pass — Unworthy (sophomoric framing) — High

The goal is not maximal output.
The goal is a visible pattern of inclusion and exclusion.

## Subscription model
Subscribers choose a specific editor, not a generic content bundle.

A subscription gives access to:
- the editor’s live judgment ledger
- weekly Confession
  - where they changed their mind
  - what they missed
  - where they remain uncertain
- monthly Audit
  - what held up
  - what degraded
  - what they would now rule differently

The product is less “newsletter from an expert” and more “ongoing access to a judgment process.”

## Fork / disagreement layer
When two editors rule differently on the same item, subscribers should be able to inspect that disagreement.

The value is not tribal sorting.
The value is visible contested judgment.

Useful forms:
- editor A passed an item editor B strengthened
- editor A broke something editor B complicated
- a subscriber can move laterally into a differing judgment path

The disagreement graph should stay bounded and legible, not become a debate platform.

## Emotional job to be done
CANON should relieve the feeling that one must personally keep up with everything.

The emotional payoff is delegated sovereignty:
- someone competent is standing watch
- the reader inherits a standard, not a feed
- the subscriber can orient quickly without opening 40 tabs

The product should make a user feel:
- less overwhelmed
- less susceptible to noise
- less dependent on algorithmic ranking
- more anchored by a discernible standard of refusal

## Design principles
### 1. Refusal is first-class value
Passes and negative rulings are not secondary metadata. They are part of the product.

### 2. Scarcity enforces standards
Editors should be constrained enough that inclusion remains meaningful.
A hard or soft daily cap on logged Reads may help prevent the ledger from becoming another firehose.

### 3. Legibility over mystique
Subscribers should see the process of judgment, not merely its outcomes.

### 4. Accountability over authority theater
Editors should disclose uncertainty, changes of mind, and misses.
The product must resist guru dynamics.

### 5. Domain specificity over general punditry
Editors are strongest when they operate in bounded areas of competence.

## What not to do
- do not turn this into a generic creator platform
- do not optimize for social clout
- do not introduce global leaderboards of “best editors” too early
- do not score editors purely by consensus alignment
- do not make the product a real-time outrage terminal

Consensus is a weak validator in many domains.
A better editorial accountability layer should include:
- visible uncertainty
- retrospection
- subscriber trust/retention
- reasoning quality over time
- domain-bounded track record

## MVP
A plausible first version includes:
- one-editor subscription pages
- live judgment ledger
- short Cases attached to selected items
- weekly Confession
- monthly Audit
- visible archive by domain/topic
- simple disagreement links between editors on the same item

Optional later features:
- taste compatibility onboarding
- side-by-side comparison of editor rulings
- private subscriber notes
- export/archive of ledger history

Do not build yet:
- algorithmic recommendation feed
- broad social graph
- public comment system
- mass-market “top editors” ranking
- institution-style credential gatekeeping as the sole basis of access

## Business model
Subscription-first.

Illustrative model:
- $12–25/month per editor
- platform take on subscription revenue
- no ads
- no clickthrough-driven incentives

The editor should be paid to judge well, not to maximize output or attention.

## Relationship to the broader hole
CANON is a public-layer answer to the judgment crisis.

If CIRCUIT is intimate small-group triage, CANON is accountable public discernment.
It proposes a way to make the labor of selection economically viable again without recreating legacy editorial institutions or surrendering to algorithmic feeds.

## Summary
CANON becomes compelling when framed not as “content from experts,” but as a market for legible, accountable judgment.

Its strongest idea is simple:
- in an age of infinite content, competent refusal is valuable enough to subscribe to.
