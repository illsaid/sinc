# Trellis — Keeper Extraction

## Purpose
Extract the genuinely useful ideas from the Trellis concept while separating them from the more ornamental or over-ideologized protocol material.

The goal is not to preserve the full Trellis pitch.
It is to retain the parts that feel productively adjacent to Sinc and the broader anti-feed / human-filtration line.

---

## Main takeaway
There is real progress in Trellis.

The strongest parts are not the random-family doctrine or the cryptographic social architecture.
The strongest parts are the anti-feed object model and the attention-history model.

The core contribution is this:
anti-feed consumption may work best when built from:
- discrete content units
- one-at-a-time encounter
- completion as the primary signal
- greyed archival memory
- slow circulation
- bounded non-predictive serendipity

That is the keeper.

---

## 1. Discrete block model
### Keep
Treat content as discrete units rather than a continuous stream.

Possible properties:
- one item at a time
- finite daily stack
- explicit encounter
- clear end state

### Why it matters
This is a real anti-feed mechanic.
It turns consumption back into bounded encounter rather than passive endless flow.

### Translation
“Block” may or may not be the final language, but the object model is strong.

---

## 2. Finish -> grey out -> archive/gallery
### Keep
Once an item is finished:
- it greys out
- it enters a permanent personal gallery / archive
- it becomes part of a visible attention history

### Why it matters
This is one of the strongest ideas in the note.
It creates memory and continuity.
It gives users something current platforms largely refuse to provide: a legible record of what actually survived their time.

### Why it is valuable
- users can search what they finished
- completion becomes visible evidence, not evaporating exhaust
- the product gains continuity without needing a feed

---

## 3. Completion as the primary signal
### Keep
Not:
- likes
- clicks
- raw views
- shallow engagement

But:
- this sustained my attention

### Why it matters
Completion is a much better signal than most ad-tech proxies.
It is closer to actual human value than click-through or short-loop compulsion.

### Best extension
Completion becomes more useful when paired with:
- recurrence / revisit
- pass-along
- short note / warrant
- argument / return

---

## 4. Slow circulation over instant virality
### Keep
Content should be allowed to move through people slowly.
Quality should emerge through:
- time
- recurrence
- re-encounter
- bounded circulation

Not primarily through:
- immediate click velocity
- viral spike behavior

### Why it matters
This principle protects:
- weirdness
- difficult work
- minority taste
- objects that need metabolization rather than instant legibility

This is one of the cleanest anti-algorithmic principles in the whole concept.

---

## 5. Solo mode first
### Keep
Launch as a personal tool first.

Possible first version:
- harvest links
- process finite stack
- greyed gallery
- anti-feed attention memory

### Why it matters
This is the most operationally sane part of Trellis.
It proves actual utility before requiring the social/protocol architecture to exist.

### Product lesson
Do not make the whole concept dependent on the network arriving fully formed.

---

## 6. Cleaner dataset idea
### Keep
“What humans finished and valued” may be a much cleaner dataset than ad-tech contaminated engagement data.

### Why it matters
Current datasets tend to conflate:
- quality
- amplification
- identity signaling
- addictive behavior
- platform distribution effects

A dataset built around:
- finish
- revisit
- pass-on
- argue-with
- keep

would be substantially more interpretable.

---

## 7. Platforms as dumb CDNs
### Keep
Platforms should not be trusted to mediate meaning.
At most, they are distribution substrate.

### Why it matters
This aligns directly with the broader anti-feed instinct:
- keep the pipe
- reject the ranking regime
- separate content transport from meaning assignment

---

## 8. Bounded randomness / serendipity
### Keep in softened form
There should be some degree of non-predictive exposure.
The system needs:
- off-axis introduction
- controlled randomness
- disciplined surprise

### Important correction
Randomness is useful for serendipity.
Randomness is not automatically useful for trust, obligation, or stable social architecture.

So keep:
- bounded serendipity
- semi-random discovery lanes
- controlled surprise

Do not overcommit to:
- random kinship as doctrine
- obligation by arbitrary assignment

---

## What to discard
These parts are mostly drag.

- random families of 80 as the primary social unit
- obligation over affinity as product dogma
- cryptographic kinship theater
- drand / ipfs / gossip / no-central-registry obsession as product identity
- “ungameable” rhetoric
- protocol maximalism
- social architecture that feels more ideological than behaviorally convincing

---

## What to reframe instead
Instead of:
- random families with obligation

Prefer:
- optional bounded circles
- rotating cohorts
- temporary validation groups
- semi-random discovery lanes
- trusted groups plus controlled serendipity

That preserves the anti-tribal / anti-deterministic insight without forcing users into artificial pseudo-kinship.

---

## Best concise formulation
Trellis’s real contribution is this:

anti-feed consumption should be built from discrete blocks, completion-based signal, greyed archival memory, slow circulation, and some bounded non-predictive serendipity.

Everything else is costume.
