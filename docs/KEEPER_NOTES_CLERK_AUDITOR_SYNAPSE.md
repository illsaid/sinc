# Sinc Keeper Notes — Clerk, Auditor, Synapse

## Purpose
Capture the strongest salvageable ideas from three additional adjacent concepts and sort them by likely usefulness for:
- standalone wedge potential
- ancillary products in the Sinc universe
- feature layers or internal mechanics that could strengthen Sinc itself

The goal is aggressive curation, not preservation for its own sake.

---

# 1. Clerk

## Overall assessment
Top-tier concept. One of the strongest ideas so far.

Clerk attacks the hole from a slightly different angle than Sinc:
- not explicit trust routing first
- but post-consumption judgment capture and taste alignment

It proposes a digital replacement for the record-store clerk / video-store employee / used-bookstore person whose real function was not authorship but situated, confidence-calibrated guidance.

## Strongest ideas
### A. The product is the lost job
This is one of the cleanest formulations in the whole exploration.

Clerk restores a lost function:
- someone who remembers what was worth your time
- someone who can say “skip this” without anxiety
- someone whose signal is contextual, not generic

### B. Permission to reject
This is a major keeper.

Much of the emotional tax of algorithmic mediation is FOMO and post-consumption hollowness.
Clerk addresses that by making “don’t bother” a product feature rather than an absence of recommendation.

### C. Binary end-of-consumption capture
Very strong mechanic.

Instead of asking for full reviews or star ratings, Clerk captures a tiny judgment at the natural stopping point:
- was it worth your time?
- optional: who is it for?

This is elegant because it:
- minimizes friction
- aligns with an existing behavioral moment
- avoids performance incentives

### D. Taste alignment over social graph
This is the biggest conceptual challenge Clerk poses to Sinc.

Sinc says:
- choose the humans you trust

Clerk says:
- discover the humans whose past judgments best predict your own

That distinction matters.
It opens the possibility that the best mediators for a user are not socially chosen in advance but discovered through comparative behavioral fit.

### E. Finite queue that ends
Another major keeper.

Clerk’s strongest emotional promise is not endless discovery.
It is:
- here are seven things worth your time this week
- when you’re done, you’re done

This is deeply aligned with the anti-feed thesis.

## Risks / caution
- “involuntary curation” is too ominous as framing
- integration assumptions (streaming, Kindle, tab-close, etc.) may be brittle in practice
- binary capture may need careful adaptation by domain
- the taste-matching engine would need to be genuinely good or the whole concept weakens fast

## Best use relative to Sinc
Three plausible roles:
1. standalone wedge: a personal anti-feed finite queue built from taste alignment
2. internal mechanic: use Clerk-like taste matching under the hood inside Sinc
3. hybrid: explicit trusted humans + inferred taste-aligned humans coexist

## Compressed keeper
Clerk’s strongest salvage is a finite anti-feed queue built from post-consumption binary judgment capture and taste alignment, with permission to skip as a first-class feature.

# 2. Auditor

## Overall assessment
Excellent diagnosis; more cautious keeper as product.

The concept is strongest when treated as judgment-audit infrastructure, not as a universal public scoreboard for human credibility.

## Strongest ideas
### A. Counterfeit judgment is the disease
This is a core keeper.

The diagnosis is not merely that judgment is scarce.
It is that counterfeit judgment has become hard to distinguish from real judgment because incentives are no longer legible.

That framing is highly valuable.

### B. Visible incentives
One of the strongest ideas in the concept.

A useful system should make legible:
- what the judge stands to gain or lose
- what incentives are shaping the claim
- whether those incentives align or distort

This is not a disqualifier layer.
It is context.

### C. Domain-specific reliability with decay
Good keeper.

Track record should be:
- narrow
- recent
- domain-specific
- decaying over time

Expertise does not transfer cleanly, and stale reliability should not be treated as current authority.

### D. Calibration matters, not just correctness
Very good.

A trustworthy judgment system should care about:
- how confident someone was
- whether that confidence was calibrated
- whether they admitted uncertainty honestly

This is much more interesting than simple “was right / was wrong” scoreboarding.

### E. “No reliable track record exists here” as a valid output
This is the strongest keeper in the second half.

The absence of trustworthy judgment, made legible, is often more useful than fake certainty.

## Risks / caution
- automatic public dossier-building is socially dangerous
- many valuable forms of judgment are not easily falsifiable or outcome-scoreable
- over-quantification would select for punditry and pseudo-predictive behavior
- as a standalone mass product it could easily rot into an epistemic score terminal

## Best use relative to Sinc / Canon
Best interpreted as:
- a conceptual layer
- an audit mechanic for public judgment products
- potentially adjacent to Canon rather than a separate first wedge

Possible role:
- Canon = visible judgment ledger
- Auditor = evidence that the ledger deserves trust

## Compressed keeper
Auditor’s strongest salvage is a framework for legible judgment quality: visible incentives, domain-bounded track record, calibration, recency, and explicit acknowledgment when no trustworthy judgment infrastructure exists.

# 3. Synapse

## Overall assessment
Strong ancillary concept. Worth keeping.

Synapse addresses the hole at the level of the hostile feed itself.
Rather than asking users to leave the habit loop, it intercepts it and replaces the algorithmic payload with a finite brief built from trusted human signal.

## Strongest ideas
### A. Replace the payload, not the habit
This is the best line in the concept.

Users continue the same muscle-memory behavior:
- open Twitter / YouTube / etc.

But instead of the platform’s infinite recommendation surface, they receive a finite, trusted replacement layer.

This is a powerful wedge because it avoids migration friction.

### B. High-friction signals only
Very strong keeper.

Good candidate signals:
- highlighted passages
- read to completion
- direct share
- maybe annotated save

This is much better than:
- likes
- views
- clicks
- shallow engagements

### C. Trust Ring
A bounded trusted group is useful here.

The exact size may vary, but the principle is good:
- a finite trusted set
- strong privacy expectation
- no open social sprawl

### D. Finite replacement digest
Another major keeper.

Instead of a scrolling feed, Synapse proposes:
- a heavily pruned daily brief
- finite set of items
- explicit completion state

This maps very closely to the broader anti-feed thesis.

### E. “Digest Complete” state
Excellent emotional mechanic.

A clear endpoint in a previously infinite environment is highly valuable.
It introduces closure where platforms intentionally remove it.

## Risks / caution
- browser/mobile interception may be technically brittle depending on platform
- local LLM synthesis should remain subordinate to human signal, not replace it
- quantified social-proof surfaces should be used sparingly or not at all
- P2P/protocol language is implementation detail, not product essence

## Best use relative to Sinc
Synapse maps cleanly as:
- a feed-replacement layer in the Sinc universe
- an ancillary product built around anti-feed intervention
- a complement to shelf/list-based Sinc

Possible relationship:
- Sinc = shelf / curation substrate
- Synapse = hostile-feed interception + finite digest replacement

## Compressed keeper
Synapse’s strongest salvage is a system that intercepts algorithmic feeds and replaces them with a finite, trusted, completion-oriented brief built from high-friction signals inside a bounded circle.

# Cross-concept ranking
## Strong standalone wedge candidates
1. Clerk
2. Synapse
3. Auditor (only if narrowed heavily and likely not first)

## Strong additions/layers to Sinc
1. Clerk-style taste alignment engine
2. Synapse-style feed interception / digest replacement
3. Auditor-style judgment-quality / incentive legibility layer

## Best individual ideas across all three
- permission to reject
- finite queue that ends
- post-consumption binary capture
- visible incentives
- explicit “no reliable judgment exists here” output
- replace the payload, not the habit
- high-friction signals only
- digest complete / closure state

## Compressed conclusion
Of these three, Clerk and Synapse feel especially alive.

Clerk is a personal anti-feed taste prosthesis.
Synapse is a structural anti-feed interception layer.
Auditor is most useful as a judgment-audit framework rather than a full standalone public product.

All three contain ideas worth keeping near the main Sinc line.
