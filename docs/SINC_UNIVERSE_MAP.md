# Sinc Universe Map

## Purpose
A compact strategic map of the Sinc universe after the hole-exploration exercise.

This file is meant to be faster to use than the full report.
It separates:
- core
- wedges
- layers
- primitives
- discard pile

---

# 1. Core
These are closest to the main Sinc line.

## Sinc Core
Primary object:
- finite domain-specific shelves / lists

Core principles:
- finite over infinite
- ledger over loop by default
- visible cardinality
- completion matters
- deliberate refill
- borrowed taste by consent
- search before recommendation
- no hidden ranking magic
- no feed logic

Likely near-term strengthening moves:
- bounded circulatory support under stalled / depleted shelves
- shared Passes / redundancy warrants
- visible provenance
- maybe hidden Clerk-style taste alignment under the hood

---

# 2. Strong wedges
These could plausibly stand alone or serve as concrete entry points into the broader universe.

## Clerk
What it is:
- a digital replacement for the record-store / bookstore / video-store clerk

Best ideas:
- permission to reject
- post-consumption binary capture
- optional “who is it for?” note
- taste alignment over social graph
- finite queue that ends

Why it matters:
- strong personal anti-feed wedge
- maybe the best challenge/complement to Sinc’s explicit-trust model

## Circuit
What it is:
- a small-group reading loop for difficult long-form material

Best ideas:
- Hold / Draw / Return loop
- 3–7 trusted people
- one item at a time
- max-capacity Hold
- structured Returns

Why it matters:
- highly buildable
- strong behavioral wedge
- likely best intimate-scale spin-off

## Canon
What it is:
- subscription to a visible editorial judgment ledger

Best ideas:
- competent refusal as product
- judgment ledger
- confession / audit formats
- refusal and exclusion as first-class value

Why it matters:
- strongest public-layer answer
- a market for legible, accountable judgment

# 3. Strong layers / ancillary products
These feel most useful as attached layers, companion products, or modes.

## Synapse
Role:
- feed interception and replacement layer

Best ideas:
- replace the payload, not the habit
- high-friction signals only
- bounded trust ring
- finite replacement digest
- “digest complete” state

Best use:
- anti-feed intervention layer sitting beside Sinc

## Gutcheck
Role:
- lightweight live-web judgment overlay

Best ideas:
- tiny glanceable human verdicts
- domain-specific trusted circles
- short in-context reasons
- “gutcheck this” interaction

Best use:
- extension wedge
- ambient human judgment layer on the live web

## Lens
Role:
- richer interpretation mode above Gutcheck

Best ideas:
- consensus + divergence
- short structured mediation context
- before-you-engage brief
- richer human context on ambiguous/high-stakes material

Best use:
- high-context mode, not necessarily primary wedge

## Horizon
Role:
- framing / daily mode for finite feed replacement

Best ideas:
- finite daily horizon
- visible count/progress
- completion signal
- permission to stop
- enoughness as product promise

Best use:
- user-facing framing layer over Synapse-like mechanics

# 4. Useful primitives
These are not full products worth pursuing in their current form, but they contain reusable pieces.

## Attestation / provenance primitive
Source concepts:
- Trustfall
- Trustster
- Vouch
- TrustNet

Keep:
- signed, scoped, revocable attestations
- visible provenance paths
- domain-specific trust receipts
- tombstones / revocation

Use cautiously for:
- provenance
- quiet domain trust receipts
- one-hop legibility

Do not expand into:
- trust scores
- graph boosts
- universal reputation systems

## Auditor primitive
Best ideas:
- visible incentives
- domain-specific track record
- calibration quality
- decay / recency
- “no reliable judgment exists here” as a valid output

Best use:
- audit layer for public judgment products
- Canon-adjacent credibility context
- maybe selective use inside Sinc ecosystem

## Axiom-like epistemic tooling
Best ideas:
- claim extraction rather than summary-first design
- structural weakness mapping
- anti-slop defense
- provenance + incentive context

Best use:
- adjacent high-stakes tooling
- not really a core Sinc child

# 5. Main design tensions to keep alive
These tensions are productive and should not be prematurely resolved.

## Chosen trust vs inferred alignment
- Sinc: chosen humans
- Clerk: discovered taste-matching

## Ledger vs loop
- Sinc: shelf-first
- critiques suggest loop under load/stall

## Positive vs negative curation
- saves matter
- but passes / redundancy detection may be more valuable

## Destination vs ambient
- some ideas want a new product
- others are strongest as overlays/interceptors

# 6. Best mechanisms across the whole universe
If only the most reusable mechanisms survive, they are:
- finite queue that ends
- explicit completion signal
- permission to skip
- post-consumption binary capture
- domain-specific trust/taste mapping
- high-friction signal capture only
- ambient browser/feed interception
- visible provenance
- consensus + divergence
- negative curation with short warrants
- explicit “insufficient judgment exists here” output

# 7. Discard pile
These are the basins to avoid.

## Generalized trust-score systems
Examples:
- Trustfall / Trustster / Vouch / TrustNet in full-protocol form

Why discard:
- social credit drift
- fake precision
- graph mysticism
- incentive corruption
- portability of distorted platform metrics

## Mass public truth-adjudication systems
Example:
- The Gavel

Why discard:
- universal truth-court logic
- tokenized juror incentives
- procedural theater
- high capture/brigading risk

## Cathedral protocolism
Any idea that tries to answer the hole with:
- giant protocol infrastructure
- scores for humans
- public ranking engines
- crypto-incentivized truth systems

This is the wrong basin.

# 8. Rough strategic ranking
## Highest-value concepts
1. Clerk
2. Circuit
3. Canon
4. Synapse
5. Gutcheck
6. Lens

## Best supporting frameworks
- Auditor
- Attestation/provenance primitive
- Horizon framing
- Axiom-style adjacent tooling

## Strongest recurring principle
The future keeper is not a better feed.
It is finite, provenance-rich, refusal-capable, domain-specific, completion-oriented mediation.
